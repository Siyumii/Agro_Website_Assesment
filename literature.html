Literature Survey

Floriculture in Sri Lanka started as an industry in 1970. It has grown substantially during the last few years to become one of Sri Lanka's major foreign exchange generating ventures. Anthurium and Orchid are the most popular tropical cut flowers which are being grown commercially for exports as well as for the local market.

Climate is a major factor that affects the reduction of harvest. Anthurium plants need a shady and moist environment to grow well. According to agricultural experts in Sri Lanka, because of the hot and dry climate prevailing nowadays, various diseases that harm the plants spread rapidly. It will also cause poor growth of plants and increase the risk of outbreak of a disease. This will lead to a huge loss of revenue, effort and time.

While going through existing researches, we found out that there are applications to detect diseases of various crops like potato, grapes, palm oil etc. There are also existing applications to monitor the growth of plantations and manage risk separately. But there are no applications which consider all the mentioned aspects in a single research.

The method that growers use currently in monitoring their plantations is the traditional naked eye observation. This method is sometimes inefficient and inaccurate. Therefore the main objective of this research is to implement a user friendly application for the growers to maintain their plantations efficiently and tirelessly.
